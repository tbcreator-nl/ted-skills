# Competitor Sweep — Output Format

Use this exact structure for every sweep report. Adapt section depth to findings — more detail for HIGH threat competitors, brief for LOW.

---

## Template

```markdown
# {Client} Competitor Sweep — {Month Day, Year}

*Prepared for: {Company Name}*
*Previous analysis: {date of last sweep, or "First sweep"}*
*Changes since last sweep marked with: **[NEW]***

---

## TL;DR — What Changed Since {Last Sweep Date}

| Competitor | Threat Level | Key Change |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **{Name}** | :red_circle: HIGH | {One-line summary of most important change} |
| **{Name}** | :yellow_circle: MEDIUM | {One-line summary} |
| **{Name}** | :green_circle: LOW | {One-line summary} |
| **New entrants** | :green_circle: LOW | {Market entry assessment} |

**{Client}'s own momentum:** {Brief summary of client's recent wins, launches, hires}

---
```

## Writing Guidelines

- **Be specific, not generic.** "ANVA 6 is live with 1 customer (Midglas)" not "ANVA launched a new product."
- **Lead with the strategic implication.** Don't just describe — say what it means.
- **Use [NEW] tags** for anything that changed since the last sweep.
- **Threat assessments are opinionated.** Take a stance. "Not urgent. Monitor quarterly." is better than "This could be a threat or not."
- **Watch items are predictive.** What would change the threat level? Give a specific trigger.
- **Skip empty checks.** If a competitor has no pricing page, don't write "Pricing: Not available." Just skip it.
- **Quote exact copy.** Headlines, CTAs, and meta tags should be verbatim.
